Origin

Built with purpose.

In 2022, I lost my father to sudden cardiac arrest. It happened without warning — no symptoms we recognised, no time to respond. That experience became the driving force behind CardioSense. Could technology have helped? Could an AI system have detected the risk earlier and given us more time?

CardioSense is my answer. A machine learning pipeline that analyses clinical measurements, lifestyle factors, and ECG signals to predict cardiovascular risk — and explain exactly why.

Data

Three signals.
One pipeline.

01
Heart Disease

Clinical measurements from hospital patients — blood pressure, cholesterol, ECG results, and exercise stress test data. The most direct window into cardiovascular health.

918 patients
02
Cardiac Failure

Lifestyle and biometric data at population scale — height, weight, blood pressure, smoking habits, alcohol consumption, and physical activity levels.

70,000 patients
03
ECG Timeseries

Electrocardiogram signals captured as 123,993 time-series data points per patient. The electrical rhythm of the heart, compressed via PCA to 50 components retaining 99.68% of information.

528 patients

Roadmap

What's next for
CardioSense.

01 — Short Term
Address Class Imbalance

Apply SMOTE oversampling to the ECG dataset to improve positive-class prediction reliability and reduce the effect of the 507:21 class ratio.

02 — Medium Term
Unified Ensemble Score

Build a meta-layer that combines predictions from all three models into a single cardiovascular risk score, weighted by each model's AUC performance.

03 — Data
Indian Demographics

Current datasets are predominantly Western in origin. Partnering with Indian hospitals for anonymised patient data would make CardioSense significantly more accurate for Indian patients.

04 — Deployment
Clinic & Gym Integration

Deploy as a free AI screening tool via REST API, integrated into clinic management software and fitness centre check-in systems across India.
